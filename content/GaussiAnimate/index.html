GaussiAnimate
Reconstruct and Rig Animatable Categories with Level of Dynamics

GaussiAnimate is designed to 1) reconstruct animatable categories (ie, clothed humans, animals) from multi-view video; 2) rig consistent 4DGS with a novel skelebones representation, which well balance the intuitive control of kinematic skeletons and the deformation fidelity of free-from bones; and 3) animate the rigged 4DGS with a Partwise Motion Matching (PartMM) algorithm, which can handle complex non-rigid deformations, in a non-parametric manner.

Abstract

Rigging animatable characters, such as quadrupeds and clothed humans, presents a fundamental challenge: balancing intuitive control with deformation fidelity. While kinematic skeletons offer intuitive control, natural surface deformations involve significant non-rigid dynamics, such as loose clothing and soft tissues, that cannot be easily captured by skeletons alone. Free-form bones, which conform closely to the surface, can effectively capture non-rigid deformations but lack the kinematic structure required for intuitive control. To combine the best of both worlds, we propose a scaffold-skin rigging system, termed skelebones, with three core steps: (1) Bones, which compress temporally consistent deformable Gaussians into free-form bones to approximate non-rigid surface deformations; (2) Skeleton, which extracts a Mean Curvature Skeleton from canonical Gaussians and refines it temporally to ensure a category-agnostic, motion-adaptive, and topology-correct kinematic structure; and (3) Binding, which binds the skeleton and bones via non-parametric partwise motion matching, synthesizing novel bone motions by matching, retrieving, and blending existing ones. Collectively, these steps compress the level of dynamics of reconstructed Gaussian sequences into compact skelebones that are both controllable and expressive. We validate our approach on both synthetic and real-world datasets, demonstrating superior reanimation performance (17.3 over LBS for unseen poses) without sacrificing reconstruction quality, especially for subjects with complex non-rigid surface dynamics. Our partwise motion matching algorithm also generalizes effectively across Gaussians and meshes, delivering competitive results in low-data scenarios (48.4 over LBS, 24.1 over GRU). Code will be made publicly available for research purposes.

Skelebone Motion Database Construction

Skelebone Motion Database Construction. Given a monocular or multi-view video, our method reconstructs a consistent 4DGS, extracts the inner skeleton via curve skeletonization and the outer free-form bones via SSDR, and combines them into "skelebones," which are then used to build a motion database.

Partwise Motion Matching

Partwise Motion Matching (PartMM). Given a novel pose of the inner skeleton as input, we animate our skelebones by synthesizing the motion of the outer skeleton via part-wise motion matching. Specifically, we first (a) take the novel pose of the inner skeleton and decompose the kinematic tree into multiple parts; (b) build a part-wise motion patch database from previously reconstructed paired inner--outer skeleton motions; (c) iteratively query the database using the novel inner pose to retrieve the most similar motion patch, and perform part alignment to rotate the outer skeleton accordingly. By repeatedly applying step (c), we obtain the final outer-skeleton motion Mout.
